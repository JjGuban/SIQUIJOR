About Siquijor!

Get to know more about Siquijor!

  • The Island of Fire

    (Isla del Fuego)

    Siquijor was named "Isla del Fuego" (Island of Fire) by Spanish explorers due to the glowing fireflies that illuminated its coastline at night. This mystical glow, combined with local folklore, gave Siquijor a reputation for mysticism and healing traditions that continue today.

  • A Spanish Legacy

    Siquijor boasts well-preserved Spanish-era churches and convents, such as the Lazi Church and Convent, built in 1884. The San Isidro Labrador Church in Lazi, made of coral stones and hardwood, is one of the country’s oldest and is a recognized National Cultural Treasure.

  • The Healing Island

    Siquijor is famous for its healers and herbal medicine traditions. Every Holy Week, local healers gather to prepare sacred potions using herbs, tree bark, and mystical rituals. The island’s spiritual heritage attracts visitors seeking healing and wellness experiences.

  • Stunning Natural Wonders

    Beyond its mystical charm, Siquijor is home to breathtaking white sand beaches, enchanting waterfalls, and vibrant marine life. Cambugahay Falls, with its turquoise pools, and the Tulapos Marine Sanctuary are among the island’s top attractions for nature lovers and adventurers alike.

Scuba Diving

Activities.

Siquijor Scuba Diving offers an unforgettable underwater adventure with crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and diverse marine life. Known for its rich biodiversity, divers can explore colorful reefs, sea turtles, and even occasional reef sharks. Popular dive spots like Paliton Wall and Sandugan Point feature stunning drop-offs and thriving marine ecosystems, making Siquijor a must-visit for both beginners and experienced divers.

Sightseeing

Activities

Sightseeing in Siquijor is a journey through breathtaking landscapes, mystical attractions, and rich cultural heritage. From the centuries-old Balete Tree with its natural spring to the majestic Cambugahay Falls, the island offers stunning natural wonders. Visit the Lazi Church and Convent, a historical gem, or enjoy panoramic coastal views from Mt. Bandilaan Viewpoint. Whether exploring hidden caves, white sand beaches, or charming local villages, Siquijor’s beauty and mystery make it an unforgettable destination.

Caving

Activities

Caving in Siquijor is an adventure into the island’s mystical underground world, where stunning rock formations, hidden chambers, and freshwater pools await. Explore Cantabon Cave, the most famous spelunking spot, featuring stalactites, stalagmites, and a natural spring inside. For thrill-seekers, Baljo Cave and other off-the-beaten-path caves offer raw, untouched beauty and a glimpse into Siquijor’s mysterious past. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced explorer, caving in Siquijor promises an unforgettable experience.

Snorkeling

Activities

Snorkeling in Siquijor offers a magical experience in crystal-clear waters teeming with marine life. Popular spots like Tulapos Marine Sanctuary and Paliton Beach boast colorful coral reefs, vibrant fish, sea turtles, and even giant clams. With calm waters and excellent visibility, both beginners and experienced snorkelers can enjoy the island’s rich underwater world. Whether exploring shallow reefs or swimming alongside tropical marine creatures, Siquijor’s snorkeling sites promise an unforgettable ocean adventure.

Hiking

Activities

Hiking in Siquijor is a journey through lush forests, scenic trails, and breathtaking viewpoints. Mt. Bandilaan, the island’s highest peak, offers a rewarding trek with panoramic views of the coastline and nearby islands. Along the way, discover hidden waterfalls, century-old trees, and local wildlife. Other trails lead to mystical caves and sacred sites, adding a sense of adventure and mystery to your hike. Whether you're a nature lover or an outdoor enthusiast, Siquijor’s hiking trails promise a refreshing escape into the island’s natural beauty.

Spelunking

Activities

Spelunking in Siquijor is an exciting adventure into the island’s hidden underground caves, filled with stalactites, stalagmites, and mysterious rock formations. The most famous, Cantabon Cave, features narrow passages, freshwater pools, and glittering mineral deposits, offering a thrilling yet rewarding experience. For those seeking more exploration, Eugenia Cave and Baljo Cave provide deeper, less-traveled routes into Siquijor’s mystical underworld. Whether you're crawling through tunnels or wading through underground streams, spelunking in Siquijor is a must-try for adventure seekers.
